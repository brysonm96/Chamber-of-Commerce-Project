White Space and Clean Design

  • byui.edu

    • The byui.edu website displays many examples of white space and clean design principles. Proper spacing around web content and functional elements of a page provides a clean professional look that allows users to conveniently browse and navitage without issues.

    Rule of Thirds

  • paypal.com

    • The paypal website is a good example for demonstrating the rule of thirds principle. Many instances are seen where the subject, whether it be a logo or some other form of element, occupys one thirds of the display, leaving more open space to the other two thirds of the screen. This creates a well-composed web page for viewers to experience.

    Visual Hierarchy (A Bad Example)

  • amazon.com

    • For this principle I decided I would demonstrate what doesn't make a good example of visual hierarchy. This amazon store page shows a barrage of products thrown at the viewer in a chaotic order with no clear or present elements of intended importance. Users may easily be lost or confused while navigating this website.
